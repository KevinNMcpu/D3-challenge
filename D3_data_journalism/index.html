Correlations Discovered Between Income and Per Capita Rate of Smokers

For this project, I chose to example a possible correlation between a state's average income and the percentage of smokers in that state.

There is somewhat of a correlation between household income and percentage of the population that smokes. It seems very likely that a poorer state will have a high percentage of smokers. But, the correlation is not as strong for the higher income states, there is a wider spread of smoking percentage.

Some cultural factors came into play. You may notice Utah as an outlier. That is likely explained by the majority of Utah's population being members of the Latter-day Saints church (Mormon), and that church prohibiting tobacco use for their members.

Virginia's high tobacco use for its income level may be explained by its historical status as a major tobacco-producing state (the so-called "Tobacco Belt").

Alaska's outlying position (high income, high tobacco use) may be caused by Alaska being a low popuation of state with a high number of well-paying oil/gas jobs.
